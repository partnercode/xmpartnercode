<h1>Beginner’s Guide: How to Place Your First XM Trade</h1>

<p>Starting your trading journey on XM in 2026 can feel overwhelming, but placing your first trade is actually simple when you understand the basic steps. This guide walks you through everything from account setup to executing your first order safely.</p>

<div class="highlight">
<strong>Key Insight:</strong> Your first trade is not about profit—it’s about learning how the market and platform work.
</div>

<h2>1. Prepare Your XM Account</h2>
<ul>
<li>Register a trading account on XM</li>
<li>Complete email and phone verification</li>
<li>Finish KYC (identity verification)</li>
<li>Fund your account or use a demo account</li>
</ul>

<h2>2. Install Trading Platform</h2>
<p>XM supports MetaTrader 4 (MT4) and MetaTrader 5 (MT5), both widely used trading platforms.</p>

<ul>
<li>Download MT4 or MT5</li>
<li>Log in using your XM credentials</li>
<li>Connect to the correct trading server</li>
</ul>

<h2>3. Understand Market Basics</h2>
<ul>
<li>Currency pairs (Forex)</li>
<li>Buy vs Sell positions</li>
<li>Spread and leverage</li>
<li>Lot size and margin</li>
</ul>

<h2>4. How to Place Your First Trade</h2>
<ul>
<li>Open MT4/MT5 platform</li>
<li>Select a trading instrument (e.g., EUR/USD)</li>
<li>Click “New Order”</li>
<li>Choose Buy or Sell</li>
<li>Set lot size (start small)</li>
<li>Set Stop Loss and Take Profit</li>
<li>Confirm the trade</li>
</ul>

<h2>6. Risk Management for Beginners</h2>
<ul>
<li>Never risk more than 1–2% per trade</li>
<li>Always use Stop Loss</li>
<li>Avoid emotional trading</li>
<li>Do not overuse leverage</li>
</ul>

<h2>7. Common Beginner Mistakes</h2>
<ul>
<li>Trading without a plan</li>
<li>Using high lot sizes</li>
<li>Ignoring risk management</li>
<li>Chasing the market emotionally</li>
</ul>

<h2>8. Demo Account vs Real Account</h2>
<ul>
<li>Demo: Practice without risk</li>
<li>Real: Actual money and emotions involved</li>
<li>Start with demo before switching to real trading</li>
</ul>
